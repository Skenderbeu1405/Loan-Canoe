The HMDA data are loan level data information about mortgages and provide nationwide data on the U.S. mortgage market. 
The data set is very deep, with nearly 50 columns, and  millions of tuples and instances for every year (i.e. action on individual level loan application taken: approved, denied, etc.) . 
The dataset covers 2007-2017, and has over 6 million tuples of data for 2017 alone). 
The Home Mortgage Disclosure Act data are a frequently used source of data for research work related to mortgages.
